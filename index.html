How GIPI Works

GIPI is a synthetic indicator measuring normalized global signal variability and temporal synchronization.

It does not ingest news, social media, external databases, or user data.

Methodology (Simplified)

What GIPI Is

GIPI is a synthetic, non-extractive reference indicator designed to reflect global information pressure at a macro level.

What GIPI Is Not

GIPI is not a news aggregator, political index, country ranking, prediction system, or decision-support tool.

How the Index Is Generated

The index is derived from normalized temporal dynamics and variability patterns over time. No external content is ingested or analyzed.

Interpretation

Higher values indicate increased global information pressure. Lower values indicate reduced pressure. Values do not imply outcomes.

Intended Use

Contextual reference, media background, academic discussion, and public information only.

Index Levels Explanation

Low (0–30)

Indicates reduced global information activity. Signals are sparse, asynchronous, and low in overall pressure.

Neutral (31–55)

Represents a normal range of global information flow. Typical variability without systemic stress indicators.

Elevated (56–75)

Indicates increased global information pressure. Higher synchronization and intensified signal activity.

High (76–100)

Represents extreme global information pressure. Rare conditions with dense, highly synchronized informational dynamics.

How to Cite GIPI (Media & Publications)

When referencing GIPI, media outlets and publications are encouraged to use the following neutral citation format:

“According to the Global Information Pressure Index (GIPI), a non-political contextual indicator reflecting global information pressure…”

GIPI should be cited as contextual background only and not as evidence, prediction, or assessment of specific countries, events, or actors.
